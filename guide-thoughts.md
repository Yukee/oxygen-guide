# Guide prioritization

Necessary-and-sufficient conditions for finding relief from oxygen (conjunctive):
1. Being aware of the effectiveness of oxygen
   1. **→ guide disseminated as widely as possible**
2. Having an effective setup
   1. **→ patients are encouraged to be persistent**
   2. **→ patients know which parameters to experiment with**
3. Having (a prescription and private / insurance access) or a DIY setup
   1. **→ patients know exactly what to get**
   2. Find the right doctor
      1. **→ patients are encouraged to switch doctors**
   3. Insurance coverage
   4. Private access
      1. **→ patiens are informed about common pitfalls (?)**

A **backfire risk** with oxygen: It prevents people from experimenting with something that aborts more quickly, such as DMT **→ highlight that DMT has the potential to abort faster**

# Takeways from patient testimonials

1. → The guide should have a dedicated "getting the prescription" section. It could cover
   1. What to say when a doctor is skeptical (specific talking points, perhaps key studies to reference, etc)
   2. What the correct prescription should look like, so the patient can spot/prevent errors
   3. [More effort] How to counter "insurance won't cover it" with location-specific knowledge about what insurance actually does cover
2. → The guide should frontload that patients should expect friction, and should encourage them to persevere.
   1. The path to expect could be something like: getting diagnosed → getting the prescription (knowing which equipment to get) → working with suppliers → using oxygen effectively.
3. The bottlenecks are different depending on the region. This makes writing a short, impactful guide difficult.
