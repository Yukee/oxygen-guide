# Internal research: oxygen breathing techniques on Clusterbusters forum

*Not for publication. Research notes compiled from the Clusterbusters forum on 2026-04-20 to inform the breathing-technique section of the oxygen guide.*

## Takeaways for the guide

Start the oxygen therapy as soon as you feel the attack coming.

Inhale as much as you can, exhale as much as you can.
Some people recommend performing an abdominal crunch on the exhale, to exhale more fully.
Some hold for a moment (counting) before the exhale. Others exhale immediately.
The consensus is that the quicker one completes the full inhale-exhale cycle, the faster and more effective the abort will be.

It's important to get pure oxygen, not "polluted" by room air:

* If you're using a mask, make sure it sits tightly on your face.
  * Some people prefer using a mouthpiece than a mask. You simply hold the mouthpiece in your mouth and breath in. No need to worry about 
* If you're using a NRB mask, you'll see that it has side valves. Those valves shouldn't let room air in when you breathe in. That's why it's recommended to block them.
  * Some people block them with their fingers on the inhale, and unblock them on the exhale (so that air is let out when they breathe out in the mask).
  * Others simply tape them. This is the recommended method. With this setup, you'll either exhale through your nose, or remove the mask to exhale through your mouth.

Other important notes:

* Tingling sensations or numbness, typically in the fingers and feet, is a normal side-effect of hyperventilation. It's not dangerous. It's actually a good sign: it means you're hyperventilating effectively.
* Experiment with the setup and find what works best for yourself. Try with a mouthpiece if a mask doesn't work well for you. If the ClusterO2 kit doesn't work for you, consider using a demand valve. Try abdominal crunches to exhale more fully. Try increasing the flow rate and breathing in and out quicker. Etc. Experimentation and persistance is key.

## Search results and summaries

The forum treats "breathing technique" as inseparable from mask/regulator setup, caffeine, posture, and post-abort routine. Most veterans say the single biggest lever is a **full, forceful exhale with an abdominal crunch** before every inhale of pure O2. Beyond that, there is no consensus — three families of technique circulate, and the most experienced users tell newcomers to experiment.

### Technique 1 — Batch's hyperventilation + "redneck" bag (the canonical protocol)

**Source:** [Batch's hyperventilation + "red neck" bag](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/4919-batchs-hyperventilation-red-neck-bag/) (posted by CHfather excerpting "Batch," April 22, 2017)

Batch (a former fighter pilot, widely cited as the forum's oxygen expert) designed this to be the fastest-aborting and most oxygen-efficient protocol. It is the technique most often linked when newcomers ask "how do I use oxygen?".

**Procedure:**
1. Hyperventilate with **room air** for 30 seconds — ten complete cycles of ~3-second deep inhale / forceful exhale.
2. On each exhale: empty the lungs, then perform an abdominal "crunch" + chest squeeze until the exhaled breath makes a wheezing sound for ~1 second. Throw shoulders back and inhale as deeply as possible. Repeat in one fluid motion.
3. On the tenth exhale, hold the crunch/squeeze until wheezing for at least 3 seconds — this expels another half-liter of CO2-rich air.
4. Inhale a full lungful of 100% O2 (from mask, demand valve, or a gas-tight reservoir bag — Batch's "redneck bag" is a kitchen trash bag + Coke-bottle mouthpiece, which has zero inhale resistance and uses only 5–7 lpm to pre-fill).
5. **Hold the O2 in the lungs for 30 seconds.**
6. Exhale with a crunch and go back to step 1. Repeat until aborted — most users feel the pain start to drain away on the 4th or 5th cycle.

**Indicators it's working:** paresthesia (tingling on face, lips, hands, feet), slight chill across the back on the O2 hold.

**Mechanism (Batch's own explanation):** hyperventilation blows off CO2 → blood pH rises (respiratory alkalosis) → chemoreceptors signal capillaries to constrict throughout the body, including around the trigeminal ganglia. The lungful of pure O2 during the high-pH state causes hemoglobin to offload CO2 and onload O2 more aggressively, producing hyper-oxygenated blood flow to the brain. Vasoconstriction + hyper-oxygenation is the claimed abort mechanism. Batch argues it is physiologically impossible to pass out while hyperventilating in this way because the brain is being over-oxygenated.

**Caveats:** Batch himself writes "you might get a little dizzy." CHfather notes Batch has reported using this with an expensive oxygen demand valve as well — the redneck bag is simply a cheap alternative with lower inhale resistance.

### Technique 2 — Racer1_NC's three-method framework

**Source:** [proper oxygen method?](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/21907-proper-oxygen-method/) (Racer1_NC, May 8, 2025 — copy-paste from a conference presentation he gave)

Racer1_NC is another heavily-referenced O2 expert on the forum (does demo videos for the CB YouTube channel). He divides breathing techniques into three, and recommends experimenting to find the one that works for a given hit.

1. **The slow rhythm and count** — complete inhale → hold for a self-defined count → exhale. The mental count occupies the mind. Uses the least oxygen.
2. **The accelerated rhythm** — complete inhale and complete exhale with no pause in between, slightly faster than normal breathing.
3. **The hyperventilate** — complete inhale/exhale cycles as fast as possible. Uses the most O2 but works when the first two fail. Racer1 says this is his personal default.

**Flow-rate rule of thumb (from same post):** if, using your preferred technique, the reservoir bag never has to catch up (you can always fill your lungs without waiting), the flow rate is sufficient. If the bag overfills, you can dial it back. Racer1 recommends a 25 lpm regulator as a starting point — "like horsepower in a car, you can never have too much but you don't have to use it all."

### Technique 3 — Reverse diaphragmatic / Ujjayi "ocean" breathing (non-oxygen abortive)

**Source:** [Sharing my cluster abortive technique: reverse diaphragmatic breathing](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/28461-sharing-my-cluster-abortive-technique-reverse-diaphragmatic-breathing/) (benford, December 10, 2025)

Not an oxygen protocol — a stand-alone breathing technique a single user claims has aborted both individual attacks and entire cycles. Included here because (a) the forum has discussed it alongside O2, and (b) the proposed mechanism (vasoconstriction via respiratory alkalosis) overlaps with Batch's.

**Procedure:**
1. (Optional but Ben says it was necessary for him) Sit in a hot bath.
2. Take 3 fast, large breaths to pre-load hyperventilation.
3. **Inhale** through the nose using Ujjayi ("ocean" or "Darth Vader") breathing, and **simultaneously pull the stomach IN** — the chest fills tight, shoulders stay dropped. This is the opposite of diaphragmatic breathing.
4. **Exhale** through the nose (leak through mouth if needed), still ocean-breathing, **pushing the stomach OUT** as air leaves.
5. Continue non-stop, steady, machine-like.

**Indicator it's working:** tingling arms and head (paresthesia) throughout the session.

**Claim:** Aborted within 10 minutes of attack onset, and ended the entire cycle within 3–5 days of use. N=1, two cycles. No O2 involved. Ben has never tried O2 so he cannot compare. CHfather comments that as an abortive it is probably no more effective than O2, but if it actually ends cycles that would be a genuinely novel mechanism.

**Discussion in the thread:** Inno notes the tingling + hyperventilation profile is "almost the exact opposite" of 100% O2 at 25 lpm. CHfather connects the vasoconstriction mechanism directly to Batch's protocol.

### Technique 4 — "Standard" deep-inhale / hold / crunch-exhale

**Primary sources:**
- [Oxygen - Breathing Technique](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/13035-oxygen-breathing-technique/) (thread from 2023, multiple veterans)
- [Am I Using My Demand Valve Correctly?](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/17233-am-i-using-my-demand-valve-correctly/) (May 2024, CHfather + Racer1_NC)

This is the default pattern new users are pointed at when they don't want to dive into Batch's protocol.

**Procedure (CHfather's formulation, repeated across threads):**
1. Full exhale with a "crunch" (abdominal squeeze) to expel as much air as possible.
2. Deep inhale of pure O2.
3. Hold for 1–2 seconds.
4. Full exhale, crunch again.
5. Repeat. One cycle is roughly 10 seconds.

Spiny's version (topic 13035) adds starting the exhale+crunch on the way to the tank so you're already CO2-depleted before the mask is on. She also stresses varying the rhythm hit to hit: "I try NOT to worry that I am doing it differently than I did for the last hit! Just pay attention to which rhythm or speed is working for that hit."

### Technique 5 — Post-abort "continuation" breathing

**Sources:** [topic 13035](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/13035-oxygen-breathing-technique/), [topic 29483](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/29483-just-got-oxygen-for-the-first-time-and-need-advice/) (Craigo, April 4, 2026)

Not a breathing pattern per se but a universal forum rule: **stay on the O2 for 5–10 minutes after the attack is aborted**, breathing normally. Users who skip this step routinely report "slap-back" or "rebound" attacks within the hour.

**Craigo's step-down variant:**
- Abort phase at 15 lpm using the standard deep-inhale/full-exhale pattern (3–5 min for him).
- Once aborted, step regulator down to 10 lpm, slow the breathing, same pattern.
- Then down to 6–8 lpm for continued post-breathing.
- Purpose: conserve oxygen while still getting the anti-rebound effect.

Spiny uses 2–4 lpm for the post phase and counts her breaths (so she doesn't need to watch a clock).

### Technique 6 — "Take all the air, release as fast as possible"

**Source:** [topic 28461 reply](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/28461-sharing-my-cluster-abortive-technique-reverse-diaphragmatic-breathing/) (Nikkk, December 28, 2025)

A self-developed room-air technique. Nikkk says she takes in all the air she can and releases it as fast as possible, repeats, and slows the rhythm as the pain fades. She reports this can abort a shadow and sometimes an attack, but has never ended a cycle with it. Included as another example of the hyperventilation-family approach without O2.

### Technique 7 — Hard cardio as an alternative

**Source:** [topic 29483](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/29483-just-got-oxygen-for-the-first-time-and-need-advice/) (FiveofClubs, Tuesday at 08:28 PM)

Not breathing technique per se, but FiveofClubs reports that for him O2 produced rebounds ("while on it it would work, but when I came off it would ramp and return") while 20+ minutes of running with heart rate near 140 aborts almost all his attacks. He speculates that people with high VO2 max get less differential benefit from supplemental O2. His father aborted with "20ish minutes deep calm breathing" on O2 — he never could replicate it.

## Cross-cutting forum wisdom

These points show up in nearly every thread and are treated as settled:

- **Speed matters.** Every thread repeats: get on O2 at first sign of an attack. Vajenna: "time is of the essence. I can almost never get rid of one past a certain point."
- **100% O2 only.** The non-rebreather or mouthpiece has to seal. Tape over or block the un-gasketed holes on a standard NRB ([CHfather, topic 27205](https://clusterbusters.org/forums/topic/27205-oxygen-no-longer-working/)).
- **Caffeine synergy is universal.** Strong cold coffee, V8 Energy, Red Bull, 5-Hour Energy — hit before or at the start of the O2 session. Multiple veterans say this alone cuts abort times significantly (Dallas Denny: 5-minute aborts).
- **Flow rate should match your technique, not the other way around.** Frenchie's mistake (topic 13035) was letting a 40-lpm bag dictate a 26-cycle-per-minute rhythm. The correct pattern is: choose a breathing technique, then set flow so the bag is full when you're ready to inhale.
- **Posture.** Two patterns appear: sit and crouch forward with head toward the feet (banano, topic 21907; CHfather reference to "looking down toward your feet"), or stand up — Shaun brearley prefers standing when hyperventilating.
- **Rocking / movement.** Spiny rocks while on O2 and says the rhythm of the rocking tends to match the rhythm of the breath that's working for that hit.
- **Low-fill tanks reduce efficacy.** CHfather (topic 27205): "O2 is less effective when tanks have less oxygen in them. This can happen when the tanks are as much as half full, and it seems to be very common at 1/3 full or less." Increasing regulator flow as the tank empties is the workaround before replacing.
- **Experiment every hit.** Spiny, Racer1_NC, BoscoPiko, jon019 all say the rhythm that worked last time may not work this time. The deep-inhale / crunch-exhale cadence is the only invariant; everything else (speed, hold time, posture) should be varied to find what fits that attack.
- **The mechanism consensus (when users articulate one):** respiratory alkalosis from CO2 washout → capillary vasoconstriction around the trigeminal ganglia + hyper-oxygenated hemoglobin delivery. This is Batch's framing and it is the only mechanistic story that shows up repeatedly on the forum.

## Takeaways for the guide

1. **Lead with the "standard" technique** (deep inhale / 1-2s hold / crunch exhale, 10-second cycles) as the default. It is the least intimidating, works for most people, and is what veterans recommend to first-timers on the forum.
2. **Present Batch's hyperventilation protocol as the "advanced / fast-abort / low-O2-consumption" option**, not as the default. It requires discipline, tolerance for dizziness, and understanding of the respiratory-alkalosis mechanism. Link to the original forum post for users who want to go deeper.
3. **Racer1_NC's three-technique framework is useful for framing the range** — slow/counted, accelerated, hyperventilate — and his "flow rate should match technique, not dictate it" rule is worth quoting directly.
4. **Post-abort breathing (5–10 min on O2 after the attack ends) is non-negotiable.** Every veteran posts this. Craigo's step-down from 15 → 10 → 6-8 lpm is a practical way to do it without burning through a tank.
5. **The forceful exhale with abdominal crunch is the one invariant** across all techniques. If the guide teaches only one thing about breathing mechanics, it should be this. Multiple users report that when nothing else was working, switching from passive exhale to a crunch-exhale cut abort times in half.
6. **Acknowledge that people must experiment.** Every expert on the forum says so. The guide should avoid prescribing a single "correct" rhythm and instead teach the user to notice what's working that hit.
7. **Paresthesia (tingling in fingers, lips, feet) is a positive indicator** for the hyperventilation family of techniques — not a warning sign. Users benefit from being told this in advance because otherwise it can feel alarming.
8. **Reverse diaphragmatic / Ujjayi breathing is interesting but N=1.** Don't recommend it as a primary abortive. If mentioned at all, it would belong in a "fringe / experimental" appendix, because the cycle-ending claim is unverified and the technique requires yoga breathing skill most users don't have.
9. **Caffeine, posture, speed to tank, and flow-rate matching belong in the same section** as breathing technique, because the forum treats them as a single integrated protocol. Separating "breathing" from these related levers would misrepresent how veterans actually use O2.
10. **Don't recommend hard cardio as a primary abortive** — one user reports it, and it's physically impossible for many patients during a 9–10/10 attack. But it's a useful mention for younger / fitter patients whose O2 is producing rebounds.
